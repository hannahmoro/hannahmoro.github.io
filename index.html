Current role

I am a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Wisconsin Madison, working at the Addiction Research Center (PI: John Curtin) where I coordinate an ongoing digital phenotyping study focused on predicting lapse risk in opioid use disorder.

I am also an Assistant Director at the Psychological Science Accelerator, an international network of psychological scientists that conducts large-scale collaborative research, and a member of the executive committee of the Society for the Improvement of Psychological Science, a professional society committed to improving methods and practices in psychological science.

Education

I received a BA in Psychology from Reed College, and an MA and PhD in Psychology and Neuroscience from Duke University.

More about me

My full last name is Moshontz de la Rocha. I go by Hannah Moshontz professionally to avoid the hassles, misspellings, and misunderstandings that result from using my entire name.
